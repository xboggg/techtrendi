## Switch Types: What Actually Feels Different

The switch is everything. It determines how your keyboard feels and sounds.

**Switches I tested:**
- Cherry MX Red (linear)
- Cherry MX Brown (tactile)
- Cherry MX Blue (clicky)
- Gateron Yellow (linear)
- Kailh Box White (clicky)

**Linear switches (Red, Yellow):** Smooth press. No bump. Gamers love these. I found them boring for typing.

**Tactile switches (Brown):** Small bump when key activates. Good compromise. Best for typing.

**Clicky switches (Blue, Box White):** Loud click sound. Satisfying but annoying to others. Use only if you live alone.

**My recommendation:** Try tactile first (Browns). Most versatile.

## The Keyboards I Tested

Budget tier ($50-80): Keychron C1, Royal Kludge RK61, Redragon K582

Mid-range ($80-130): Keychron K8, Ducky One 3, Leopold FC900R

Premium ($130-200): Keychron Q1, Mode Sonnet, GMMK Pro

**Winner for most people:** Keychron K8 ($89). Wireless. Hot-swappable switches. Mac and Windows compatible. Just works.

**Winner for typists:** Leopold FC900R ($119). Best build quality. Quietest typing. No RGB nonsense.

**Winner for gamers:** Ducky One 3 TKL ($109). Fast response. Durable. RGB if you want it.

## What Doesn't Matter (Marketing Nonsense)

**"Gaming keyboard" labels:** Marketing. Any mechanical keyboard works for gaming.

**RGB lighting:** Looks cool for 2 weeks. Then you turn it off because it's distracting.

**Polling rate above 1000Hz:** You cannot perceive the difference. 1000Hz is plenty.

**"Optical switches faster than mechanical":** True but irrelevant. The difference is 0.2 milliseconds. Your reaction time varies by 50+ milliseconds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are mechanical keyboards worth it?**

If you type more than 2 hours per day, yes. The comfort and durability justify the cost.

**Q: What's the best switch for typing?**

Tactile switches (Cherry MX Brown or Gateron Brown). Clicky if you don't mind noise.

**Q: Can I use a gaming keyboard for work?**

Yes, but choose one without flashy RGB and loud clicky switches unless you work alone.
