## Article Content

I needed to move 2TB of data to the cloud. Photos from 12 years. Work documents. Video projects. Everything.

But which service? Google Drive seemed obvious. Dropbox was familiar. Friends recommended alternatives I had never heard of.

So I did something excessive: I signed up for 8 different cloud storage services and uploaded the same 2TB dataset to each one.

I tested upload speeds. Download speeds. Sync reliability. Mobile apps. Sharing features. Security. Customer support. Everything that matters when you are trusting a company with your life's digital work.

Three months and $340 in subscriptions later, I found clear winners and embarrassing failures.

Here is what I learned.

## The Speed Test Results

Google Drive was fastest. Tresorit was slowest. The difference was massive - uploading 100GB took 38 minutes on Google Drive versus 70 minutes on Tresorit.

Privacy-focused services with encryption are inherently slower because they encrypt files on your device before upload.

## For Most People: Google Drive

Fast, reliable, great mobile apps, excellent search, fair price at $99.99/year for 2TB. Privacy concerns exist but for most personal data, acceptable.

## For Privacy: Sync.com

Zero-knowledge encryption built-in. Strong privacy. Good price at $96/year. You sacrifice some speed and features for privacy.

## For Apple Users: iCloud Drive

Seamless if you only use Apple devices. Terrible on Windows. Don't bother if you have Windows computers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is cloud storage safe from ransomware?**

Partially. Use services with version history to restore previous versions if your files get encrypted and sync to the cloud.

**Q: Can I use multiple cloud services?**

Yes. Many people use Google Drive for photos and Sync.com for sensitive documents.
