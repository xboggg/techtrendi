## Common VPN Marketing Lies

### "Military-grade encryption"

Meaningless marketing term. All reputable VPNs use AES-256. This is standard, not special.

### "We don't log anything"

Many claim no-logs but haven't been independently audited. Look for third-party audits.

### "Blocks 100% of ads and malware"

VPN-based ad blocking is basic. Dedicated ad blocker (uBlock Origin) works better.

### "Increase your speed"

VPNs always decrease speed due to encryption overhead and routing. Cannot make connection faster than base speed.

### "Thousands of servers in 90+ countries"

More servers doesn't mean better. Quality and location of servers matters more than quantity.

## When You Actually Need a VPN

**Use VPN when:**
- Public WiFi (coffee shops, hotels, airports)
- Accessing content blocked in your region
- Downloading torrents
- Living in country with heavy censorship
- Accessing work network remotely
- Want to hide browsing from ISP

**Don't need VPN for:**
- Banking (already encrypted with HTTPS)
- Shopping (already encrypted)
- General browsing (HTTPS encrypts most traffic)
- Complete anonymity (VPN is not anonymity tool)

VPNs shift trust from ISP to VPN provider. Choose provider carefully.

## What Did NOT Work

**Free VPNs:** Terrible speeds, sell data, inject ads, monthly data limits. Not worth it.

**VPN browser extensions:** Most are proxies, not VPNs. Don't encrypt all traffic, only browser traffic.

**Built-in VPNs (Opera, etc):** Usually just proxies with limited functionality. Use dedicated VPN instead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a VPN?**

Depends on threat model. Essential for public WiFi, useful for privacy from ISP, necessary in censored countries. Not essential for general home browsing with trustworthy ISP.

**Q: Are free VPNs safe?**

No. Free VPNs make money by selling your data or injecting ads. Use paid VPN from reputable provider.

**Q: Can VPN make me anonymous?**

No. VPN hides IP from websites you visit, but you're still identifiable through cookies, browser fingerprint, login accounts. For anonymity, use Tor.

**Q: Will VPN slow my connection?**

Yes, always. Good VPNs reduce speed 20-30%. Bad VPNs reduce 50%+.

**Q: Can Netflix detect VPN?**

Yes. Netflix blocks many VPN IPs. ExpressVPN and NordVPN work most reliably for streaming.
