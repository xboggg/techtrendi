## Performance Testing Results

I tested 15 popular games on both systems at their native resolutions.

**Desktop (1440p):**
- Cyberpunk 2077: 78 fps (High settings)
- Fortnite: 187 fps (Epic settings)
- Call of Duty MW3: 142 fps (High settings)
- Baldur's Gate 3: 94 fps (Ultra settings)

**Laptop (1080p):**
- Cyberpunk 2077: 89 fps (High settings)
- Fortnite: 203 fps (Epic settings)
- Call of Duty MW3: 156 fps (High settings)
- Baldur's Gate 3: 112 fps (Ultra settings)

The laptop has higher framerates, but it's rendering 44% fewer pixels. When I set the desktop to 1080p, it crushed the laptop in every game.

## The Real Differences That Matter

Performance is not the full story. Here's what actually affected my gaming experience.

### Heat and Noise

**Desktop:** Whisper quiet. GPU rarely exceeded 68°C. Could game with headphones off.

**Laptop:** Jet engine. GPU hit 85°C regularly. Fans maxed out constantly. Required headphones.

### Upgradeability

**Desktop:** Swapped GPU in 15 minutes. Added more RAM easily. Huge advantage.

**Laptop:** Nothing upgradeable except RAM and storage. GPU and CPU are soldered.

### Portability

**Laptop:** Took to friend's house for LAN parties. Played in bed. Brought on trips.

**Desktop:** Sits on desk. Never moved it once in 6 months.

For most people, pick desktop for performance. Pick laptop only if portability matters to you specifically.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can gaming laptops match desktop performance?**

At the same price, no. A $1,500 laptop loses to a $1,500 desktop by 30-40% in gaming performance.

**Q: How long do gaming laptops last?**

3-4 years before feeling outdated. Desktops last 5-7 years with GPU upgrades.
