## What Actually Matters for Video Editing

**RAM: Get 32GB minimum, 64GB ideal**

I tried editing with 16GB. Constant crashes. 32GB was stable. 64GB was smooth. More than 64GB only helps if editing 8K or heavy effects.

**Storage: Fast NVMe SSD is critical**

SATA SSD: Project lagged
NVMe SSD: Smooth playback
Multiple drives: Even better (OS on one, footage on another)

**GPU: Matters more than you think**

Adobe Premiere and DaVinci Resolve use GPU heavily for effects and export. RTX 4060 Ti is the sweet spot.

**CPU: Important but not everything**

6 cores minimum. 8 cores ideal. More than 12 cores shows diminishing returns unless you do heavy 3D work.

## My Recommendation

**For 1080p editing:** Budget build ($800-900)

**For 4K editing:** Mid-range build ($1500-1700)

**For 8K or professional work:** High-end build ($2500-3000)

Don't overspend on components you won't use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Mac or PC for video editing?**

PC gives better performance per dollar. Mac has better software integration. Both work well.

**Q: How much RAM do I really need?**

32GB for 4K, 64GB for 4K with heavy effects, 96GB+ for 8K.

**Q: Do I need a high-end GPU?**

For Premiere and Resolve, yes. RTX 4060 Ti is minimum I'd recommend for serious 4K work.
