## What Actually Matters

### Resolution Sweet Spot

1080p: Fine for 24" monitors. Pixelated on 27".
1440p: Perfect for 27". Best balance.
4K: Beautiful but requires powerful GPU.

**Winner: 1440p on 27" monitor.** Sharp without GPU strain.

### Refresh Rate Diminishing Returns

60Hz: Fine for office work. Gaming feels sluggish.
144Hz: Huge upgrade. Noticeable immediately.
240Hz: Marginal improvement. Only for competitive esports.
360Hz: Marketing gimmick. Can't perceive difference from 240Hz.

**Winner: 144Hz.** Jump from 60Hz to 144Hz life-changing. 144Hz to 240Hz barely noticeable.

### Panel Type Matters

**IPS:** Best colors, best viewing angles, more expensive
**VA:** Best contrast (deep blacks), good colors, slower response
**TN:** Fastest response, terrible colors and viewing angles

**Winner: IPS for most people.** VA if you watch movies and want deep blacks.

Returned all TN panels. Colors terrible. Viewing angles so bad that tilting head changes colors.

### Response Time Marketing Lies

Manufacturers claim "1ms" everywhere. It's misleading.

Real-world response times tested with high-speed camera:
- TN panels: 3-5ms
- IPS panels: 5-8ms (despite claiming 1ms)
- VA panels: 8-12ms (despite claiming 4ms)

Anything under 10ms fine for gaming. Don't obsess over 1ms claims.

### Curved vs Flat

Curved better for ultrawides (34"+). Flat better for 27" monitors and photo/video editing.

## Monitors I Kept

### For Gaming: Dell S2721DGF ($350)

27" 1440p 165Hz IPS. Perfect size and resolution. Excellent colors. Good response time. Best all-around monitor.

### For Productivity: LG 34GN850-B ($650)

34" ultrawide 1440p 144Hz IPS. Massive screen space replaces two monitors. Gaming immersive. Productivity significantly better.

### For Content Creation: BenQ SW270C ($550)

27" 1440p 60Hz factory calibrated. Excellent color accuracy (dE<2). Hardware calibration support.

### Budget Pick: AOC 24G2 ($180)

24" 1080p 144Hz IPS. Unbelievable value. IPS panel at budget price with good stand.

## Monitors I Returned

**ASUS ROG Swift PG279QM ($700):** 240Hz not noticeably better than 165Hz. Not worth $350 more.

**Samsung Odyssey G9 ($1100):** Too big. Neck strain. Many games don't support 32:9 aspect ratio.

**Samsung Odyssey G7 ($500):** Aggressive curve weird on 27". VA black smearing in dark scenes.

## What Did NOT Matter

**HDR:** Most monitors fake HDR. True HDR requires 600+ nits and local dimming. Under $1000, HDR is gimmick.

**RGB lighting:** Distracting after one week.

**Built-in speakers:** Always terrible.

## Features That Matter

- Ergonomic stand (height, tilt, pivot, swivel)
- VESA mount compatibility
- USB-C with power delivery
- Multiple inputs (HDMI and DisplayPort)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is 4K worth it for gaming?**

Not for competitive gaming. 1440p better balance of sharpness and performance. 4K beautiful for single-player games if you have powerful GPU.

**Q: Do I need 240Hz?**

Only for competitive FPS. 144Hz sufficient for 95% of gamers.

**Q: Ultrawide or dual monitors?**

Ultrawide for immersion and clean desk. Dual monitors for flexibility. I prefer ultrawide.
