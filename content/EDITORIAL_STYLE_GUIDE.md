# TechTrendi Editorial Style Guide

## Purpose
This guide ensures all TechTrendi content feels authentically human, passes AI detection, ranks well on Google, and builds reader trust. Every writer must follow these standards.

---

## 1. VOICE & TONE

### The TechTrendi Voice
- **Knowledgeable Friend**: Write like you're explaining tech to a smart friend over coffee
- **Confident but Not Arrogant**: Share expertise without talking down
- **Honest & Direct**: No corporate speak, no beating around the bush
- **Slightly Opinionated**: Take stances, have preferences, show personality

### Tone Spectrum
```
Too Formal ← ← ← [TechTrendi Sweet Spot] → → → Too Casual

"One must consider..." ✗
"You should consider..." ✓
"Here's the thing—you gotta think about..." ✓
"lol just do whatever" ✗
```

### Example Transformations

**Too Robotic:**
> "It is important to note that password security is a critical component of online safety."

**TechTrendi Style:**
> "Here's the thing about passwords: most people think they're being clever. They're not. Hackers have seen every trick you can imagine."

**Too Casual:**
> "So like, passwords are super important and stuff lol"

## 2. ANTI-AI DETECTION STRATEGIES

### Sentence Rhythm (Critical)
Never write 3+ sentences of similar length in a row. Mix aggressively:

**Bad (AI Pattern):**
> "Password managers store your credentials securely. They generate strong passwords automatically. They sync across all your devices. They fill in login forms instantly."

**Good (Human Pattern):**
> "Password managers changed my life. Seriously. I went from reusing the same three passwords everywhere (don't judge—you probably do it too) to having unique 20-character monsters for every single account. The best part? I don't have to remember any of them."

### Banned Phrases (AI Tells)
Never use these—they scream "AI wrote this":

| Banned | Use Instead |
|--------|-------------|
| "In today's digital age" | "Right now" / "These days" / Just start the point |
| "It's important to note that" | Just state the thing |
| "Furthermore" | "And" / "Plus" / "Here's another thing" |
| "Moreover" | "Also" / "On top of that" |
| "Additionally" | "And" / "Plus" |
| "In conclusion" | "So here's the bottom line" / "Look—" |
| "Delve into" | "Look at" / "Explore" / "Dig into" |
| "Navigate" (metaphorically) | "Figure out" / "Deal with" / "Handle" |
| "Leverage" | "Use" |
| "Utilize" | "Use" |
| "Facilitate" | "Help" / "Make easier" |
| "Plethora" | "Ton" / "Bunch" / "Lots" |
| "Myriad" | "Many" / "Lots of" |
| "Comprehensive" | "Complete" / "Full" / Just remove it |
| "Robust" | "Strong" / "Solid" |
| "Seamless" | "Smooth" / "Easy" |
| "Cutting-edge" | "Latest" / "Newest" / Be specific |
| "State-of-the-art" | Just describe what makes it good |
| "Revolutionary" | "Really good" / Be specific |

### Required Human Elements
Include at least 3 of these per article:

1. **Personal anecdote**: "I once spent 4 hours troubleshooting this exact issue..."
2. **Rhetorical question**: "Sound familiar?"
3. **Mild frustration**: "It's honestly ridiculous that companies still..."
4. **Admission of imperfection**: "I'll be honest—I still mess this up sometimes"
5. **Casual transition**: "But here's the thing..." / "Now, here's where it gets interesting..."
6. **Direct reader address**: "You've probably experienced this" / "If you're like most people..."
7. **Opinion statement**: "Honestly, I think this is overkill for most people"
8. **Parenthetical aside**: "(and trust me, I've tried them all)"

### Vary Paragraph Lengths
Mix short punchy paragraphs with longer explanatory ones:

```
This is wrong. [Too short alone]

[Long paragraph with lots of detail that explains everything in exhaustive
detail and covers all the nuances and caveats and exceptions and special
cases that might apply to different situations.] [Too long without breaks]

This approach actually works. [Short punch]

Here's why: when you vary your paragraph lengths, your writing develops
rhythm. It breathes. Readers can scan the short bits and dive deep into
the meaty sections when they're ready. [Medium explanation]

Big difference. [Another short punch]
```

## 3. ARTICLE STRUCTURE

### Opening Hook (First 100 Words)
Every article MUST start with one of these:

1. **Relatable Frustration**
   > "Your phone is at 3%. You're nowhere near a charger. And your Uber driver keeps calling because they can't find you. We've all been there."

2. **Surprising Statistic**
   > "73% of people reuse passwords. I know because I used to be one of them—until I watched a hacker break into accounts live on YouTube."

3. **Story Teaser**
   > "Last month, my friend lost $12,000 because of a single text message. It looked completely legitimate. Here's how the scam worked..."

4. **Contrarian Take**
   > "Everyone tells you to buy the latest iPhone. They're wrong. Here's why a $300 phone might actually be better for you."

5. **Direct Problem Statement**
   > "You clicked on this article because your computer is painfully slow. Good news: we're going to fix that today. Most of these solutions take under 10 minutes."

### Never Start With:
- "In this article, we will discuss..."
- "Today, we're going to explore..."
- "Welcome to our guide on..."
- Generic definitions ("A password manager is a tool that...")

### Section Structure
```
## H2 Heading (Every 300-400 words)

Opening sentence that hooks into this section.

Main content with specific details, examples, numbers.

Transition or mini-conclusion for this section.

### H3 Subheading (Optional, for longer sections)

More detailed breakdown.

**Pro Tip**: Call out especially important info like this.
```

### Article Flow Template
```
1. HOOK (100 words)
   - Emotional connection or problem statement

2. CONTEXT (200-300 words)
   - Why this matters
   - What most people get wrong

3. MEAT (1,200-1,500 words)
   - Main content in 4-6 sections
   - Each section: problem → solution → proof
   - Include specific examples, numbers, screenshots

4. ACTION STEPS (200-300 words)
   - Clear next steps
   - Recommended tools/products (affiliate)

5. WRAP-UP (100-150 words)
   - Reinforce key point
   - Encourage action
   - NOT "In conclusion..."
```

## 4. SEO GUIDELINES

### Keyword Usage
- **Primary keyword**: In title, first 100 words, 2-3 H2s, naturally throughout
- **Don't force it**: If it sounds weird, rewrite the sentence
- **LSI keywords**: Include related terms naturally
- **Never keyword stuff**: If you notice the keyword appearing every paragraph, thin it out

### Title Formula
```
[Emotional Hook/Problem] + [Promise/Solution] + [Optional: Timeframe/Number]

Examples:
✓ "Stop Wasting Money: The Best Phones Under $300 That Actually Last"
✓ "Why Is My Computer So Slow? The Real Fixes Nobody Tells You"
✓ "I Tested 12 VPNs So You Don't Have To: Here's What Actually Works"

✗ "Best Budget Phones 2026 - Top 10 Cheap Phones Review Guide"
✗ "How to Fix Slow Computer: Complete Guide to Speed Up PC"
```

### Meta Description Formula
150-160 characters that:
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Create curiosity or promise value
- Include a call to action

```
"Tired of your phone dying by noon? These 8 fixes actually work—#4 doubled
my battery life. Takes 5 minutes to set up."
```

### URL Slug Rules
- Lowercase, hyphenated
- 3-5 words maximum
- Include primary keyword
- No dates (evergreen URLs)

```
✓ /best-budget-phones-300
✓ /fix-slow-computer-guide
✗ /the-complete-ultimate-guide-to-fixing-your-slow-computer-in-2026
✗ /best-phones-jan-2026
```

## 5. FORMATTING STANDARDS

### Typography
- **Bold** for key terms, product names, important warnings
- *Italics* for emphasis, introducing new concepts, slight asides
- `Code formatting` for technical terms, file names, commands
- > Blockquotes for quotes, key takeaways, important callouts

### Lists
Use sparingly and only when truly needed:

**Good use (scanning checklist):**
> Before you buy a new phone, check:
> - Battery health (Settings → Battery)
> - Storage usage (what's actually full?)
> - Whether your carrier has upgrade deals

**Bad use (lazy writing):**
> VPNs are good because:
> - They encrypt your traffic
> - They hide your IP
> - They let you access geo-blocked content
> - They protect on public WiFi

(This should be prose with examples, not a list)

### Numbers & Data
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Always use numerals for: percentages, prices, specs, measurements
- Include specific numbers: "47% of users" not "many users"

### Product Mentions (Affiliate)
Integrate naturally as solutions, not ads:

**Good:**
> "After trying six different options, I landed on NordVPN. It's not the cheapest ($3.99/month if you commit to 2 years), but the speeds were consistently 20% faster than ExpressVPN in my tests."

**Bad:**
> "I recommend NordVPN. Click here to get NordVPN with our special discount! NordVPN is the best VPN."

## 6. FACT-CHECKING & ACCURACY

### Required Verification
- All statistics must have a source (can be linked or mentioned)
- Product specs must be current (check manufacturer site)
- Prices must be approximate or dated ("around $299 as of January 2026")
- Software steps must be verified on current versions

### Handling Uncertainty
When you're not 100% sure:
- "In most cases..."
- "This varies by device, but generally..."
- "Your experience may differ, but typically..."

Never state uncertain things as absolute facts.

## 7. IMAGE GUIDELINES

### Image Requirements Per Article
- **Minimum**: 4 images
- **Hero image**: Required, 1200x630px (OG share size)
- **In-content**: 800x450px minimum

### Image Style
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Real people, real settings | Sterile stock photos |
| Slightly imperfect/lived-in | Over-produced studio shots |
| Natural lighting | Heavy HDR or filters |
| Diverse representation | Generic models |
| Action/emotion | Static poses |
| Actual screenshots | Mockups (unless showing concept) |

### Alt Text
Descriptive and useful for accessibility:
```
✓ alt="Person frustrated looking at slow-loading laptop screen"
✗ alt="computer image"
✗ alt="slow computer laptop technology speed loading"
```

### Image Sourcing
- Unsplash, Pexels for stock photos
- Screenshots (your own, current)
- Canva for infographics/comparisons
- Credit photographers when required

## 8. INTERNAL LINKING RULES

### Per Article Requirements
- **3-5 internal links** to other TechTrendi articles
- **1-2 tool links** to TechTrendi tools where relevant
- **Contextual anchor text** (descriptive, not "click here")

### Link Placement
- First internal link within first 300 words
- Spread throughout, not clustered
- Link at moment of relevance

### Anchor Text Examples
```
✓ "Check your current password strength with our free tool"
✓ "I covered this in detail in my password security guide"
✓ "...which is why your VPN choice matters (here's my tested recommendations)"

✗ "Click here for more information"
✗ "Read this article"
✗ "Learn more"
```

---

## 9. CALLS TO ACTION

### Throughout Article
- Soft CTAs to tools: "Try it yourself with our free [tool name]"
- Related content: "If you found this useful, you'll want to check out..."

### End of Article
Every article ends with clear next steps:

```
## What to Do Next

1. [Specific first action based on article content]
2. [Tool recommendation with link]
3. [Related article for deeper dive]

Questions? Drop them in the comments—I read every one.
```

### Never End With:
- "Thanks for reading!"
- "I hope you found this helpful"
- "Don't forget to share!"
- Just stopping mid-thought

---

## 10. QUALITY CHECKLIST

Before publishing, verify:

### Content Quality
- [ ] Opening hook in first 2 sentences
- [ ] No banned AI phrases
- [ ] Varied sentence lengths
- [ ] At least 3 "human elements" included
- [ ] Personal anecdote or experience mentioned
- [ ] Specific numbers/data (not vague claims)

### SEO
- [ ] Primary keyword in title, first 100 words, and 2+ H2s
- [ ] Meta description under 160 characters
- [ ] URL slug is clean and keyword-rich
- [ ] 3-5 internal links placed naturally

### Formatting
- [ ] H2 every 300-400 words
- [ ] Paragraphs vary in length
- [ ] Bold used for key terms
- [ ] Lists used sparingly and appropriately

### Images
- [ ] Minimum 4 images
- [ ] Hero image 1200x630
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] No obvious stock photo vibes

### Final Read
- [ ] Read aloud—does it flow naturally?
- [ ] Would you actually read this if you found it on Google?
- [ ] Does it answer the search intent completely?
- [ ] Is there anything you'd be embarrassed to publish?

---

## Remember

The goal isn't to trick Google or fool readers. It's to write genuinely useful content that happens to be optimized. If you focus on helping the reader, the rest follows.

Write like you're talking to a friend who needs help. Because you are.
